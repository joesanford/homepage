More about me

When I was four years old, both computers and video games entered my life. I still remember the Christmas getting my NES, and the following birthday when I was finally allowed to play on our old 386. From there, it was all downhill.

As I got older, I became more and more interested in technology. A result of this was learning more as well. By the time I was six years old, I had surpassed my father in working knowledge of our computers. When I was eight, I built my own PC for my own use from the ground up.

When I first entered college at Penn State in 2003, I was not sure what I truly wanted to study. I was thinking of both Computer Engineering and Information Science and Technology. While I started with the former, I soon changed to the latter. After two years of college, I took time working in the IT field to gain a better perspective of what I wanted my career path to become.

Returning to undergrad, I hit the ground running and spent more time in research labs. I began working on research in Cognitive Science, where it became apparent that I could mix my passions of development and music together. I headed to Tufts planning on getting my Masters while further strengthening my skills and knowledge.

In my spare time, my hobbies include circuit bending, hacking, playing guitar and banjo, video games, skiing, golf, watching Jeopardy!. I also spend a lot of my spare time on various projects.
